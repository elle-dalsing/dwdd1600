Who Signed It?

Famous Signers

Some of the most famous signers of the Declaration of Independence include:

Thomas Jefferson
The principal author of the Declaration of Independence and later the third President of the United States.
John Adams
A key advocate for independence who later became the second U.S. President.
Benjamin Franklin
A renowned diplomat, scientist, and statesman who helped secure French support for the revolution.
John Hancock
President of the Second Continental Congress, known for his bold and prominent signature.
Samuel Adams
A strong proponent of colonial independence and leader of the Sons of Liberty.
Richard Henry Lee
Introduced the resolution calling for independence from Britain.
Roger Sherman
A member of the committee that drafted the Declaration and one of the few to sign all major U.S. founding documents.
Robert R. Livingston
A member of the Committee of Five who helped draft the Declaration but did not sign it.
Charles Carroll of Carrollton
The only Catholic signer and the last surviving signatory of the Declaration.

These signers of the Declaration of Independence played vital roles in shaping the foundation of the United States. Their courage and commitment to the cause of liberty helped ignite a revolution that led to the formation of a new nation. Beyond simply signing their names, many of them continued to serve as leaders, diplomats, and visionaries who influenced the course of American history. Their legacy endures as a testament to the principles of freedom and self-governance that continue to define the nation today.
